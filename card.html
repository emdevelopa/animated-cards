Invisalign Treatment

Invisalign Treatment

Invisalign is a popular alternative to traditional braces to help you straighten your teeth and give you that beautiful smile you've always wanted. Developed by Align Technology, Invisalign consists of clear treatment trays that gently (and discreetly) move your teeth over time. It's a highly effective orthodontic treatment for mild to moderate crowding and spacing issues. The retainers are worn for 22 hours per day, allowing plenty of time to eat and clean your teeth.

Dental Implants

Dental Implants

Dental implants are a great long term solution for people who have lost one or more of your natural teeth. The implants are made of a strong and durable material that integrates with your jawbone. This allows the implant to function like a natural tooth and withstand the forces of chewing. A crown or bridge is attached to the implant to create a natural-looking tooth replacement. While dental implants are more costly initially than other dental treatments, they are a long-term solution that can last for many years - enhancing your smile and oral health along with protecting bone health.

Wisdom Teeth Removal

Wisdom Teeth Removal

Wisdom teeth are permanent molars that usually erupt in the late teens or early 20s. Unfortunately, they are often impacted and don't have the room to grow properly. Most people choose to undergo wisdom teeth removal to protect their dental health. Without extraction, impacted wisdom teeth can lead to a number of long term problems, including pain, infection, gum disease, damage to nearby teeth and even cysts in the worst case scenario. They are also prone to decay, which can further spread to other healthy teeth. Our dentists provide comfortable, expert care for wisdom teeth, using local anesthetic or conscious sedation if needed. The surgery takes about 30 minutes to an hour. Our dentists can perform a thorough exam to help determine if wisdom teeth removal is the right choice for you.

Dental Crowns

Dental Crowns

Dental crowns are tooth-shaped caps that are placed over a damaged tooth to protect it and restore its function. Made to match the color of your teeth, dental crowns are often completely invisible. They are made of ultra-strong, tooth-like materials that can withstand biting and chewing forces. This makes them a good option for teeth that have been weakened by decay, a large filling, or a root canal, restoring the function of a damaged tooth. Consult with one of our expert dentists to decide if a dental crown is the best choice for you.
